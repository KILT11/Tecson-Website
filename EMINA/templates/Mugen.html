Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba - The Movie: Mugen Train

Episdoe 1: Mugen Train

Tanjiro Kamado, along with his sister Nezuko (who is a demon but fights alongside him), Zenitsu Agatsuma, and Inosuke Hashibira, board the Mugen Train. They are accompanying Kyojuro Rengoku, the Flame Hashira (one of the highest-ranking Demon Slayer swordsmen), to investigate a series of mysterious disappearances. On the train, they encounter Enmu, Lower Rank One of the Twelve Kizuki (the most powerful demons under Muzan Kibutsuji). Enmu ha s a sinister plan to plunge the passengers into deep sleep and destroy their spiritual cores, thus incapacitating them. Enmu's Blood Demon Art allows him to manipulate dreams. The Demon Slayers are trapped inillusory dream worlds, where they experience their deepest desires and regrets. Tanjiro, for instance, dreams of reuniting with his deceased family. Tanjiro, with Nezuko's help, manages to break free from the dream state. He then works to awaken his comrades. Tanjiro and Inosuke engage in a fierce battle with Enmu. Enmu reveals that he has fused himself with the train itself, making him a formidable and difficult opponent to defeat. They eventually manage to sever Enmu's neck, but the train is still out of control. As the immediate threat of Enmu subsides, a new and even greater danger emerges: Akaza, Upper Rank Three of the Twelve Kizuki, appears. Akaza seeks to challenge Rengoku, impressed by his immense power, and offers to turn him into a demon. Rengoku refuses Akaza's offer and engages him in a devastating battle to protect the passengers. Despite his incredible strength and skill, Rengoku is ultimately mortally wounded by Akaza. Rengoku's unwavering resolve and sacrifice leave a profound impact on Tanjiro, Inosuke, and Zenitsu. He imparts his wisdom and encourages them to continue their path as Demon Slayers. Akaza flees as the sun begins to rise, escaping the only weakness of demons. The film concludes with the Demon Slayers mourning Rengoku's death and grappling with the heavy loss, while also honoring his bravery.
